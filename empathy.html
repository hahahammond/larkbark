EMPATHY GAME LIBGUIDE

I created a LibGuide on a subgenre of video games known as “empathy games” as part of an assignment for LIS-407 at Simmons (Spring 2016).


An ‘empathy game,’ simply stated, is a video game that inspires empathy in its players. Empathy games may produce cathartic experiences (whether they are specifically designed to do this or not), operating as “other people simulators” (in the words of game developer Lucas Pope) that offer players intimate insights into the experiences of unfamiliar people in unfamiliar situations.


My guide is intended to provide an overview of empathy games that may be of interest to students, staff, and faculty at an institute of higher education. The games I have highlighted in the “Notable Games” section are intended to represent a wide variety of themes and developers. I have also included a mix of games with high production values and simple browser games to illustrate that games need not rely on hyperrealistic graphics and sound effects in order to elicit empathy in the player.


In drawing on this guide as a sort of “empathy bank,” members of an academic community could reference these games in either academic research or as tools for understanding and acceptance of “the other” in addressing social and personal issues experienced on campus.
